🚨 Emergency Protocols Quick Reference

⚠️ CRITICAL REMINDERS
• Call 911 early, not late
• Airway first - can't breathe = dead
• BVM for: laryngospasm, hypoventilation, oversedation, cardiac arrest, bronchospasm (if severe), aspiration (if severe), choking (if severe)
• NO PULSE = START CPR IMMEDIATELY
• AED: Resume CPR immediately after shock
• Place CPR Board under patient for cardiac arrest
• If unable to intubate AND unable to ventilate:
   → Emergency cricothyrotomy + Call 911

🫁 AIRWAY EMERGENCIES

Laryngospasm (Crowing/Stridor)
Signs: Bucking respiratory pattern, crowing sounds · BVM + Succinylcholine 20-40mg
▼
1. Call 911
2. Remove objects, suction airway (also with Laryngoscope if possible)
3. Semi-supine, positive pressure O₂ with BVM (100% at 6L/min)
4. If no relief: 1-2mL Normal Saline transtracheal
5. Last resort: Succinylcholine 20-40mg IV (1mg/kg IM) → Assist ventilation with BVM
Succinylcholine Syringe (3cc, label "SUCC"):
IV Route:
• 20 mg → 1.0ml
• 40 mg → 2.0ml

IM Route (if no IV): 1mg/kg = 0.05mg/lb
• 150 lb: 7.5 mg = 0.375ml
• 200 lb: 10 mg = 0.5ml
• 250 lb: 12.5 mg = 0.625ml
Calculate: Weight in lbs × 0.05 ÷ 20 = mL

• Storage: Keep refrigerated
6. Maintain Airway / Continue to assist ventilation
7. Prepare to intubate / Intubate if necessary (advanced airway)
8. If unable to intubate or ventilate, prepare to perform emergency cricothyrotomy
9. Prepare to manage bradycardia (due to hypoxia)
⚠️ SUCCINYLCHOLINE WARNING: Causes temporary apnea. Be prepared to ventilate with BVM and manage airway.
Bronchospasm / Asthma (Wheezing)
Signs: Wheezing, cough, dyspnea · Albuterol 2 puffs
▼
1. Upright position
2. Albuterol 2 puffs STAT, repeat q10-20min
3. O₂ to >94%
4. If severe: Epinephrine 0.3-0.5mg Sub-Q, repeat q20min up to 1mg
Epinephrine Syringe (3cc, label "EPI"):
• 0.3 mg → 0.3ml
• 0.5 mg → 0.5ml
5. 911 if unresolved
6. Reverse sedative PRN
7. If aspiration suspected: Diphenhydramine 50mg IV + Dexamethasone 20mg IV
Diphenhydramine Syringe (3cc, label "BENADRYL"):
• 50 mg → 1.0ml
Dexamethasone Syringe (3cc, label "DEXA"):
• Concentration: 10mg/ml
• 20 mg = 2.0ml
8. If unable to ventilate: Use BVM with 100% O₂
9. If unable to ventilate, prepare for intubation with Succinylcholine 20mg IV and Call 911
Hyperventilation
Signs: Rapid breathing, lightheaded, tingling hands · Reassure + paper bag
▼
Reassure patient
1. Terminate procedure
2. Remove objects from oral cavity
3. Attempt to calm the patient, DO NOT GIVE O₂
4. Have patient breathe into their cupped hands over the mouth and nose to reduce CO₂ elimination, if unresolved after 10 minutes
Hypoventilation (Shallow/Slow Breathing)
Signs: SpO₂ dropping, slow/shallow breaths · BVM + Flumazenil or Naloxone
▼
1. Call patient's name
2. Head tilt/chin lift/jaw thrust, analyze patient's breathing (ensure monitors are properly placed)
3. Suction (use OPA/NPA if needed)
4. If not breathing adequately: Use BVM to assist
5. Reverse sedative if needed:
• Flumazenil for Versed (see Oversedation - Benzo)
• Naloxone for Fentanyl (see Oversedation - Opioid)
Emesis / Aspiration
Signs: Vomiting, gagging, aspiration · Trendelenburg + suction + Zofran 4mg IV
▼
For Emesis/Vomiting:
Give Ondansetron 4mg IV over 2-5 minutes
Ondansetron Syringe (3cc, label "ZOFRAN"):
• 4 mg → 2.0ml
For Aspiration:
1. Head down / Turn to side
2. Suction oral cavity IMMEDIATELY
3. BVM with 100% O₂ if needed
4. If severe aspiration:
• Diphenhydramine 50mg IV
• Dexamethasone 20mg IV
• Succinylcholine 20mg IV (if airway compromise)
Diphenhydramine Syringe (3cc, label "BENADRYL"):
• Concentration: 50mg/ml
• 50 mg = 1.0ml
Dexamethasone Syringe (3cc, label "DEXA"):
• Concentration: 10mg/ml
• 20 mg = 2.0ml
Succinylcholine Syringe (3cc, label "SUCC"):
• 20 mg → 1.0ml
• Storage: Keep refrigerated
5. Call 911
6. Intubate if necessary
7. Order Chest X-ray
Choking / Airway Obstruction
Signs: Cannot speak/cough, cyanosis, silent chest · Heimlich maneuver
▼
Conscious Patient:
1. Heimlich maneuver / Back blows
2. If fails → Direct visualization, Magill forceps removal
Unconscious Patient:
1. Lay flat, call 911
2. Direct laryngoscope, suction, Magill forceps
3. BVM if needed
4. Cricothyrotomy if total obstruction
⚠️ If foreign body not recovered, order X-ray
🔧 Advanced Airway Devices
▼
NPA (Nasopharyngeal Airway) - Use WITH BVM
When: BVM difficult, tongue obstruction · Size 6-7 adults
▼
Sizing (3 sizes available):
• Small (6.0-6.5mm): Small adults, measure nostril to earlobe
• Medium (7.0-7.5mm): Average adults
• Large (8.0-8.5mm): Large adults
• General rule: Diameter = patient's pinky finger width
1. Select appropriate size (nostril to earlobe measurement)
2. Lubricate generously with water-soluble gel
3. Check nostril patency (choose more open nostril)
4. Insert with bevel toward septum (middle of nose)
5. Advance gently along floor of nose (aim toward ear, NOT upward)
6. If resistance: STOP, try smaller size or other nostril
7. Advance until flange rests on nostril
8. Resume BVM ventilation with NPA in place
⚠️ Contraindications:
• Severe facial trauma
• Suspected basilar skull fracture
• Known bleeding disorder or on anticoagulants
LMA (Laryngeal Mask Airway) - If BVM + NPA Fails
When: BVM + NPA failing · Size 3-4 adults
▼
Sizing (3 sizes available):
• Size 3: Small adults (30-50kg / 66-110lb) - 4-5 feet tall
• Size 4: Average adults (50-70kg / 110-154lb) - 5-6 feet tall
• Size 5: Large adults (70-100kg / 154-220lb) - Over 6 feet tall
• When in doubt: Choose larger size
Preparation:
1. Select appropriate size based on height/weight
2. Completely deflate cuff (press flat against hard surface)
3. Lubricate back surface (cuff side) generously
4. Remove dentures if present
Insertion:
5. Head in sniffing position (extended neck, flexed head)
6. Hold LMA like a pen, cuff tip against hard palate
7. Press firmly along roof of mouth and advance
8. Use index finger to guide along hard palate
9. Advance until resistance felt (cuff in hypopharynx)
10. Inflate cuff without holding device:
   • Size 3: 20mL air
   • Size 4: 30mL air
   • Size 5: 40mL air
11. LMA should lift slightly (~1-2cm) when properly seated
Confirmation:
12. Attach BVM to LMA connector
13. Ventilate and confirm:
   • Chest rise bilateral
   • Breath sounds equal both sides
   • No air leak around cuff
   • Capnography if available (square waveform)
✓ If successful: Continue ventilation via LMA
✗ If unsuccessful: Try KING LT or prepare for cricothyrotomy
KING LT AIRWAY - If LMA Fails or Alternative
When: LMA failed · Size 3 most adults
▼
Select size: Size 3 (most adults 4-5ft), Size 4 (adults 5-6ft), Size 5 (adults >6ft)
1. Lubricate device
2. Head in neutral/sniffing position
3. Insert with curved tip along hard palate
4. Advance until base of connector aligns with teeth/gums
5. Inflate cuff with syringe (follow device markings)
6. Confirm placement - chest rise, breath sounds bilateral
7. Connect to BVM and ventilate

❤️ CARDIAC EMERGENCIES

❤️ Ischemia / Pressure
Signs: Crushing chest pain, radiates to arm/jaw · Nitroglycerin 0.4mg SL
▼
Angina / Chest Pain
Signs: Crushing chest pain, radiates to arm/jaw · Nitroglycerin 0.4mg SL
▼
1. Stop procedure
2. Supine
3. O₂ 4L/min via nasal cannula (titrate to >94%)
4. Nitroglycerin 0.4mg SL (repeat q5min, max 3 doses)
⚠️ NITRO CONTRAINDICATIONS:
• Viagra/Cialis/Levitra in last 24hr
• BP <90 mmHg systolic
• HR <50 BPM
If yes to any: NO NITRO
5. Place EKG monitor / Check all vitals
6. After 2nd Nitro dose: Call 911
7. Aspirin 325mg CHEWED
8. If pain continues: Fentanyl 25mcg or N₂O
Fentanyl · 3cc · BLUE tape · 25mcg = 0.5ml
9. Activate ACLS protocol if indicated
Myocardial Infarction (HEART ATTACK)
Signs: Severe chest pain, diaphoresis, arm/jaw radiation · Call 911 + Aspirin 325mg
▼
1. CALL 911 IMMEDIATELY
2. Supine position
3. O₂ 4L/min via nasal cannula (titrate to >94%)
4. Aspirin 325mg CHEWED (not swallowed!)
5. Nitroglycerin 0.4mg SL (q5min, max 3 doses if no contraindication)
6. Fentanyl 25mcg IV for pain (q10-20min)
Fentanyl Syringe (3cc, BLUE tape):
• 25 mcg → 0.5ml - Same as IV sedation syringe
7. Monitor vitals / Place EKG
8. Prepare for cardiac arrest - have AED ready
Hypertension / HBP (>180/120)
Signs: BP >180/120 · Midazolam 1-2mg if anxiety
▼
Stop procedure.
1. Give Midazolam 1-2mg (if secondary to anxiety)
Midazolam Syringe (1cc, ORANGE tape):
• 1 mg → 0.2ml
• 2 mg → 0.4ml
2. Patient in Supine Position
3. Give 100% O₂ and reassess patient
4. Check all monitor parameters
5. Give Labetalol 20mg IV over 2 minutes (maximum effects seen in 5 minutes)
Labetalol Syringe (3cc, label "LABETALOL"):
• Concentration: 5mg/ml
• 20 mg = 4.0ml - need 2 syringes (2.0 ml each)
• Give slowly over 2 minutes
Hypertensive Crisis (>180/110)
Signs: BP >180/110, severe headache, vision changes · Labetalol IV
▼
1. Call 911
2. Supine position
3. O₂ to >94%
4. Labetalol 20mg IV over 2 minutes, repeat 20-40mg q10min, up to 300mg total
Labetalol Syringe (3cc, label "LABETALOL"):
• Concentration: 5mg/ml
• 20 mg = 4.0ml — 2 syringes (2.0 ml each)
• 40 mg = 8.0ml — 3 syringes (2.0–3.0 ml each)
5. Monitor vitals continuously
Hypotension (Low BP)
Signs: Dizzy, SBP <85, pale · Atropine or Ephedrine IV
▼
1. Trendelenburg / Supine
2. IV fluids wide open
3. O₂ to >94%
4. Check heart rate:
If Bradycardic (HR <60): Atropine 0.5mg IV (q3-5min, max 3mg)
Atropine Syringe (3cc, label "ATROPINE"):
• Concentration: 1mg/ml
• 0.5 mg = 0.5ml
• 1.0 mg = 1.0ml
If Normal Heart Rate (60-100): Ephedrine 2.5-5mg IV
Ephedrine Syringe (3cc, label "EPHEDRINE"):
DILUTION REQUIRED: Mix 1mL of 50mg/mL Ephedrine with 9mL of saline = 5mg/mL (need 4 syringes total - 1 for draw, 3 for dilution)
• 2.5 mg → 0.5ml
• 5 mg → 1.0ml
Effects in 10 minutes, duration 4 hours
If Tachycardic (HR >100):
• Consider vagal maneuvers first
• Phenylephrine 0.1mg (100mcg) IV
Phenylephrine Syringe (3cc, label "PHENYL"):
• 100 mcg → 1.0ml
5. Monitor vitals
6. Call 911 if unresponsive
⚡ Arrhythmias (With Pulse)
Signs: HR <50 BPM, low BP · Atropine 0.5mg IV
▼
Symptomatic Bradycardia (<50 BPM)
Signs: HR <50 BPM, low BP · Atropine 0.5mg IV
▼
1. Evaluate ABCs / ECG monitor
2. Secure Airway if needed
3. Open IV all the way to allow fluids to run in
4. Give Supplemental O₂ titrate to >94%
5. Determine Rhythm (Sinus Brady? Heart Blocks?)
6. If Symptomatic Bradycardia (with hypotension), Treat the Heart Rate:
Give Atropine 1mg IV, repeat q3-5 min, max 3mg total
Atropine · 3cc · label "ATROPINE"
• Concentration: 1mg/ml
• 1mg = 1.0ml · give as IV bolus
• Repeat q3-5 min · max 3mg (3.0ml) total
7. Call 911
8. If unresponsive to Atropine — Push-dose Epinephrine (bridge to EMS):
Push-dose Epinephrine · 3cc · label "EPI"
Prepare: Add 1mg Epi (1ml of 1:1000) to 100ml NS → 0.01mg/ml (10 mcg/ml)
Dose: Draw 1ml · give 0.5–1.0ml IV push (5–10 mcg) q2-3 min
• Titrate to response · watch HR and BP
• Patient has IV line — use existing access

Signs: Sudden HR >150, palpitations · Vagal maneuver → Adenosine 6mg IV
▼

2. Supplemental O₂

• 6 mg → Use prefilled 6mg/2mL syringe
• 12 mg → Use prefilled 12mg/4mL syringe

OPTION 2 - Draw into 3cc syringe (3mg/ml concentration):
• 6 mg = 2.0ml - use 1 syringe
• 12 mg = 4.0ml - need 2 syringes (2.0 ml each)
⚠️ Must give RAPID IV PUSH (1-2 seconds)
4. Flush Adenosine with 10mL of NS in IV line immediately

💀 Cardiac Arrest (No Pulse)
CPR + AED · Epinephrine 1mg IV
▼
V-Fib / Pulseless V-Tach (CARDIAC ARREST)
CPR + AED · Epinephrine 1mg IV
▼
1. Call for Help and Call for AED
2. CPR + AED · Epinephrine 1mg IV (30:2, 100-120/min)
Lower chair, lay patient flat, place CPR Board, begin 2-person CPR
3. Turn on the AED and follow instructions
4. If shockable rhythm: Shock, then IMMEDIATE CPR for 2 min
5. Establish Airway and appropriate ventilation
6. Start IV
7. After 2nd shock: Epinephrine 1mg IV push — 1ml ampule (1mg/ml · 1:1000), repeat q3-5min
Epinephrine · label "EPI"
• 1mg IV push = draw entire 1ml ampule (1mg/ml · 1:1000)
• Repeat q3-5 min · no max during active arrest
8. After 3rd shock: Amiodarone 300mg IV PUSH, then 150mg in 3-5min
Amiodarone Syringe (3cc, label "AMIO"):
• Concentration: 50mg/ml
• 300 mg = 6.0ml - need 2 syringes - IV PUSH
• 150 mg = 3.0ml - IV PUSH
9. Give Cardiac Lidocaine 0.6mg/lb IV push, repeat in 3-5 minutes
Cardiac Lidocaine Syringe (3cc, label "CARDIAC LIDO"):
Use 20mg/ml concentration, dose: 0.6mg/lb
• 150 lb: 90 mg = 4.5ml
• 200 lb: 120 mg = 6.0ml
• 250 lb: 150 mg = 7.5ml
Calculate: (Weight in lbs × 0.6) ÷ 20 = mL
10. Continue with CPR and AED instructions until EMS arrives
Treatable causes (H's and T's):
• Hydrogen ion (acidosis)
• Hyperkalemia
• Hypothermia
• Hypovolemia
• Tamponade
• Tension pneumothorax
• Thrombosis (coronary)
• Thrombosis (pulmonary)
PEA and Asystole (Non-Shockable Rhythms)
CPR → Epinephrine 1mg IV q3-5min
▼
1. Confirm Rhythm / ensure monitors properly connected
2. Call for Help and Call for AED
3. Begin Chest Compressions (CPR)
4. Lower chair, lay patient flat, place CPR Board, Begin two person CPR (100-120 bpm, One breath every 5-6 seconds)
5. Turn on the AED and follow instructions
6. Establish Airway and appropriate ventilation
7. Start IV and administer Bolus 500mL NS
8. Review possible causes (H's and T's - see above)
9. Give Epinephrine 1mg IV push — 1ml ampule (1mg/ml · 1:1000), repeat q3-5 min
Epinephrine · label "EPI"
• 1mg IV push = draw entire 1ml ampule (1mg/ml · 1:1000)
• Repeat q3-5 min · give as soon as IV access established
⚠️ Atropine is NOT indicated for PEA/Asystole
Removed from ACLS guidelines since 2010. Epinephrine only for non-shockable rhythms per AHA 2025.

🧠 NEUROLOGICAL

Seizure
Signs: Tonic-clonic movements, LOC · Protect airway + Midazolam 5mg IM if >5min
▼
1. Remove objects / Protect patient from injury
2. Supine position
3. If seizure >5min: Midazolam 5mg IM/IV
Midazolam Syringe (1cc, label "VERSED"):
• 5 mg → 1.0ml
4. If continues: Diazepam 5-10mg IV/IM
Diazepam Syringe (3cc, label "DIAZEPAM"):
• 5 mg → 1.0ml
• 10 mg → 2.0ml
5. Call 911
6. Post-ictal:
• Head to side, suction, O₂, monitor, comfort patient
• Observe patient for 1 hour before discharge
• Do NOT allow patient to drive home
• Refer to PCP for follow-up
Syncope (Fainting)
Signs: Sudden unconsciousness, dizziness, pallor · Ammonia Capsule
▼
1. Supine / Trendelenburg
2. Remove objects
3. O₂ to >94%
4. Ammonia capsule / sternal rub
5. Monitor vitals
6. Cold towel to forehead / reassure patient
7. If not conscious within 120 seconds → Call 911
8. Give patient 20 minutes to recover before discharge
Stroke
FAST: Face droop, Arm weakness, Speech · Call 911 — Time = Brain
▼
1. CALL 911 IMMEDIATELY
2. Supine or recovery position
3. O₂ as needed
4. Monitor vitals
5. Note time of symptom onset (critical for treatment)

Phlebitis
Signs: Edema, warmth, tenderness along vein · Remove IV + warm compress + elevate
▼
⚠️ Remove all rings immediately
1. Remove catheter
2. Elevate the extremity / limit use in affected limb
3. Warm moist towel to the area 20 min, 3-4 times per day
4. NSAID for pain
5. Few days resolution to full resolution in 3-4 weeks
6. Fever or malaise or general patient dissatisfaction are indication to consult / refer to a vascular surgeon

Signs: Pulsatile red blood, severe pain · Cardiac Lidocaine IV

1. Remove needle immediately
2. Apply pressure dressing
3. No further treatment needed — monitor patient
If medications HAVE been given:
1. Leave needle/catheter in place
2. Give slow Cardiac Lidocaine IV through catheter to relieve arterial spasm
Cardiac Lidocaine Syringe (3cc, label "CARDIAC LIDO"):
Concentration: 20mg/ml
Give slowly through catheter to relieve arterial spasm
3. Remove needle
4. Apply ice pack to area
5. Transport patient to ED immediately

7🌙 Bedtime Premedication optional▼

Diazepam (Valium)
▼
Given night before appointment to reduce anticipatory anxiety and improve sleep.
Typical Dosing:
• Healthy adult: 5 mg
• Hyporesponder / severe anxiety: 10 mg
• Hyper-responder: 0–2.5 mg
• Elderly (>65 years): ≤2.5 mg
• If taking 2 CNS depressants daily: use 2.5 mg
• If taking 1 CNS depressant daily: use 5 mg
📋 Patient Instructions
• Take at bedtime the night before appointment
• Take with small sip of water
• No alcohol
• Do not take other sedatives or sleep medications
• Go directly to sleep after taking medication
OSA risk
Patient has documented OSA. Bedtime diazepam contraindicated due to significant airway risk.
🚫 Do NOT Give If:
• Patient must drive or work before appointment
• Patient taking 3 or more CNS depressants
• Patient takes a bedtime insomnia drug
• Drug interaction with diazepam (check Epocrates & print/save before prescribing)
• Significant airway risk (OSA, etc.)

PHASE 2 — ORAL SEDATION MEDICATION
Pre-Op Oral Anxiolytic Doses
▶

•💊 Preoperative Oral Dose

Triazolam (Halcion) - Most Common
▼
Given 30-90 min pre-op for anxiolysis. Counts as CNS depressant - use conservative IV titration.
• 0.125 mg: Elderly, smaller patients, cautious first-time use
• 0.25 mg: Typical anxious adult
• 0.5 mg: Only in healthy, larger, very anxious patients (rare)
Lorazepam (Ativan) - Alternative
▼
• 0.5 mg: Elderly or cautious start
• 1 mg: Common adult anxiolysis
• 2 mg: Very anxious healthy adult (use caution with IV overlap)
💡 USE LORAZEPAM Instead of Triazolam When Patient Takes:
• Clarithromycin or Erythromycin (antibiotics)
• Antifungal medications (ketoconazole, itraconazole, fluconazole)
• HIV medications (ritonavir, indinavir, nelfinavir)
These drugs inhibit CYP3A4 and increase triazolam levels unpredictably. Lorazepam is metabolized differently and safer with these medications.
Hydroxyzine (Vistaril) - Non-Benzo Alternative
▼
• 25 mg: Mild anxiolysis
• 50 mg: Stronger sedation (common adult dose)
Onset: 15-30 min | Duration: 4-6 hrs
No reversal agent
Hydroxyzine is an antihistamine. There is NO reversal agent like benzos (Flumazenil). Effects must wear off naturally over 4-6 hours.
💡 USE HYDROXYZINE When:
• History of benzodiazepine abuse
• Severe respiratory compromise (COPD, severe OSA)
• Doctor wants lighter sedation with less respiratory depression
• Allergy component (antihistamine properties beneficial)
• Patients who have trouble breathing through their nose
• Patients who commonly experience nausea/vomiting with medication
• Severe gaggers
• The nicotine addict

2💨 N₂O/O₂ ON▼

💡 N₂O Purpose
Start with N₂O for mild anxiolysis before IV catheter placement. Helps patient relax and reduces discomfort during venipuncture. Typically 30-50% N₂O mixed with O₂.

3💉 IV Start▼

📋 IV Catheter Placement
Preferred sites: Dorsal hand, forearm (avoid antecubital fossa for mobility). Use 22g catheter for adults. Secure with transparent dressing. Flush with saline and attach D5W maintenance fluid.

4🔄 N₂O/O₂ OFF → O₂ 100% ON▼

💡 Switch to 100% O₂
Turn off N₂O but keep 100% oxygen flowing before IV sedation drugs. Ensures optimal oxygenation during sedation and provides safety margin if respiratory depression occurs.

🚨 Reversal Agents — Emergency Use Only
📋 Flumazenil process
1. Draw up 2cc of medication
2. Open IV line all the way, administer 2cc slowly over 20–30 seconds
3. Wait 3 minutes, then assess patient response
4. If signs of improvement → stop — do not administer more
5. If no improvement after 3 min → repeat dose (2cc) & wait 3 minutes
6. Up to 5 doses (1mg · 10cc) max total
7. If max given → call for help
8. Support airway · be ready for any emergency · give Naloxone if needed
9. Monitor continuously — effects last ~1 hour, but benzodiazepines last much longer; patient may re-sedate as Flumazenil wears off
10. (If patient does not respond — it is not the Versed, it is whatever else they took)

📋 Naloxone process
1. Give 0.4 mg IV slowly over 2–3 minutes
2. Wait 3 minutes — if no response, repeat dose
3. Stop once respiratory rate and depth return to safe level
4. If no response after cumulative 5–10 mg → stop and consider alternative diagnosis (stroke, encephalopathy)
5. Monitor continuously for 1–2 hours after last dose — half-life ~30 min, patient may re-sedate as Narcan wears off while opioid remains active
6. Illicitly manufactured fentanyl may be resistant — more doses and longer response time may be needed
